Parents Gateway

Parents Gateway is a web and mobile app that strengthens school-home partnership to support students in their education journey. Developed together with the Ministry of Education, the product is currently in alpha and will soon be moving into beta.

The Challenge

Teachers in Singapore are overly bogged down by administrative matters in their day to day work. This takes away from their ability to focus on their core job â€“ education.

Thus, the question for us was: How might we help teachers reduce their time spent on administrative work?

Discovery

From there we dived into the various administrative matters a teacher has to deal with across the school year.

After a few session with various teachers, we found that sending announcements and collecting consent forms from parents was the most time consuming activity (most frequent across the year + difficulty).

Today, teachers will distribute physical copies of a consent form or announcement to students to pass to their parents. Most forms will require a parent's signature. This leads to multiple issues including lost forms, mismatched schedules and so on, causing teachers to spend a lot of time chasing for them.

The form is in there somewhere

Thus, we decided on digitalising the process of sending and collating consent forms (+ school announcements) to help teachers reach parents directly for official matters.

Given that the teachers will be dealing with a large amount of information, we choose to first develop a web app for schools. On the other hand, given the frequent and unpredictable nature of school annoucements, a mobile app for parents was the most efficient way to reach them.

Design

I slowly transitioned into the project from another and started by working closely with the product manager to understand the upcoming tickets and requirements. Over time, the app was designed incrementally through rounds of iterations.

Throughout the way, we revised and refined the overall flow, cutting away many features for a more focused product.

Key Design Challenge #1:

How might we inform parents that we will be retrieving their child's information atuomatically and protect individuals with secret children.

In alpha, parents had to input their child's IC number individually. This was primarily due to the fear that individuals with children their spouses are unaware of might have their secrets revealed. As the government, it is not in our interest or intention to break families apart.

Thus, when we made the call to retreive the child's information with a tap of the button, we intentionally added a page to inform them before doing so.

Key Design Challenge #2:

How might we cater to parents who just wants to get things done without sacrificing the needs of parents who want to see more?

From our research, we identified four groups of Singaporean parents:
The kiasu â€“ who wants too much information.
The proactive â€“ who will comb through everything the school sends.
The reactive â€“ who wants sufficent information.
The disengaged â€“ who just want to get the necessary tasks done.

We wanted to ensure that our product did not just serve parents who had the luxury of time but also the ones who are busy making a living (mostly the reason why they are disengaged). Hence, we made a point to have a to-do list as one of the main navigation items.

Key Design Challenge #3:

How might we empower parents to reply to teachers without resulting in more administrative work for them?

Currently, we are exploring how we could allow parents to send messages to schools without increasing the workload of the teachers. What we have found was that the reasons for communication today seem to revolve around set topics like the child being on medical leave and so on.
